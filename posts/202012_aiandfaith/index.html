    A Visit to Chick-fil-a#

    Quite a few months ago, I met up with an old friend at a Chick-fil-a. We somehow ended up on the topic of metacognition, or thinking about thinking.

    My perspective on the matter was that it is impossible for an intelligence to be completely aware of its own though process, but it was rather hard to communicate the idea.

    The best thing I could do was to explain how you can’t fit a full carton of fries inside of another full carton of fries. Honestly not my best analogy, but hey, I think it got the idea across.

    IMAGE HERE

    So what does it mean? Well the illustration basically says “No recursive metacognition!”

    An intelligence can’t know every single part of its thought process in real time, because that would require it to model its intelligence within its own intelligence, which would just be turtles all the way down. Instant stack overflow.

    This implies that it is impossible for an intelligence to truly explain itself down to the base elements of its cognition. Thus, explanation is only possible from a higher level of abstraction.

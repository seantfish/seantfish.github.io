    Distant socializing

    Disclaimer: This post is more about the day-to-day of social media, not the organizational capabilities it provides. Also, this post does not contain rigorous research as of now.

    Social Media bad#

    The common opinion on the interwebs nowadays seems to be that social media is bad for you. The irony of spreading this message on social media platforms is not lost. I held a version of this position for a while. The extent to which some people document their lives seems somewhat excessive, to the point where they value the photo op over the actual experience. However, today I thought about something that alters my perspective to be a bit more positive about social media and the role it plays in our social lives.

    This isn’t to say that social media can’t cause issues regarding mental health. Posts on social media tend to highlight the more high intensity parts of life, either very happy or occasionally rather sad. Additionally, some platforms use user retention methods which can result in behavior akin to addiction (this isn’t unique to social media, looking at you, loot boxes).

    The Social Function of Stories#

    I think the quarantime has make me a little more sensitive to the social function social media can serve.

    When people run into each other in real life, there is a spontaneous interaction (ideally). A light conversation, and a brief life update. However, we humans have transcended the physical distances that the universe imposed on us. At least with regards to communication.

    Over text, we have to make a conscious effort to talk to each other. We gained the ability to talk to each other whenever and wherever, but at these distances, we still have not regained the ability to run into each other.

    This is the area of social behavior where social media stories fit in. They give brief interesting life updates to those in our social circle. They provide prompts for communication, filling the niche that is filled by randomly running into each other in the real world.
